# Integral — Competitive Landscape

Oppo research for positioning **Integral** in the personal development / life tracking space. Last updated: July 2026.

---

## What Integral is competing for

Users who want to:

- Log progress across **multiple life areas** (not just mood or habits)
- Stay consistent on **low-energy days** (rating + save in under a minute)
- See **patterns and maintenance gaps** without hiring a coach
- Keep data **local and private** (no account, no cloud lock-in)
- Optionally track **structured fitness programs** with real progression semantics

---

## Competitive map (by category)

### 1. “Life OS” / all-in-one platforms

| Product | Platform | Strengths vs Integral | Weaknesses vs Integral |
|---------|----------|----------------------|------------------------|
| [Benji](https://www.benji.so/) | Web, iOS, Android, desktop | Massive feature surface; sync everywhere; social/family sharing | Overwhelming; subscription; cloud-dependent; not coaching-focused on life-area balance |
| [AuraOne](https://www.auraone.app/) | Web / workspace | 8 life pillars + calendar, tasks, notes, AI meetings | Team/productivity skew; not a simple daily check-in tool |
| [HALO](https://halo.fit/) | Web | 6 modules (health, wealth, goals, home, family); habit grids | Heavy platform; freemium; not offline desktop-first |
| [Orakemu](https://orakemu.com/) | Web / mobile | Gamification (RPG roles, XP); AI summaries | Game layer may feel wrong for burnout seasons; cloud |

**Integral angle:** One calm desktop app for *reflection and maintenance*, not replacing your entire productivity stack.

---

### 2. Wheel of Life / balance trackers

| Product | Platform | Strengths vs Integral | Weaknesses vs Integral |
|---------|----------|----------------------|------------------------|
| [LifeWheel](https://lifewheel.us/) | iOS, Android | Classic 8-sphere wheel; habits tied to spheres; monthly reassessment | Mobile-only; premium for AI plans; no free-form journal depth per area |
| [worklifebalance.app](https://worklifebalance.app/) | Web, mobile | Balance wheel from *accumulated logs*; 10+ trackers | Web-first; less narrative/journal; no desktop exe |
| [Sinqly](https://sinqly.app/) | Web | AI coach + wheel; continuous scoring | Cloud; assessment-quiz feel; not local-first |

**Integral angle:** Wheel/radar charts from your real daily logs, plus checklists, metrics, notes, and guidance — on your machine.

---

### 3. Low-friction mood & micro-journaling

| Product | Platform | Strengths vs Integral | Weaknesses vs Integral |
|---------|----------|----------------------|------------------------|
| [Daylio](https://daylio.net/) | iOS, Android | 2-tap entries; Year in Pixels; activity–mood correlations | Mood/activity centric; not multi-domain life plan; mobile |
| [Exist](https://exist.io/) | Web | Aggregates Fitbit, sleep, etc.; statistical correlations | Passive/quantified-self; subscription; not holistic intention logging |
| Five Minute Journal | Mobile | Guided prompts; simplicity | Fixed template; not customizable life categories |

**Integral angle:** Daylio-level friction for *your* categories (Money, Body, Burnout, Search, Spiritual, etc.) with richer structure when you have energy.

### 4. Journaling (local / open source)

| Product | Platform | Strengths vs Integral | Weaknesses vs Integral |
|---------|----------|----------------------|------------------------|
| [Mini Diarium](https://github.com/fjrevoredo/mini-diarium) | Desktop (Tauri) | Encrypted, local-only, OSS, beautiful | Journal-only; no life-area dashboards or coaching |
| [Journaler](https://github.com/davidglassman/journaler) | Desktop (Electron) | Markdown files; privacy | No ratings/checklists/guidance |

### 5. Fitness / calisthenics progression (partial overlap)

| Product | Platform | Strengths vs Integral | Weaknesses vs Integral |
|---------|----------|----------------------|------------------------|
| [CC Tracker](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/convict-conditioning-tracker/id952514500) | iOS | Paul Wade CC steps; forum | Mobile; IAP; fitness only |

## Where Integral wins

1. **Integral life model** — Not just mood, habits, or a one-time wheel quiz. Eight intentional domains with editable checklists and metrics.
2. **Burnout-aware UX** — Rating + Save is enough; everything else is optional.
3. **Maintenance intelligence** — Declining trends, neglected areas, burnout signals, category playbooks — not just charts.
4. **Contribution grid** — At-a-glance consistency like GitHub; click any day to explore.
5. **Fitness with semantics** — Official progression tables (CC, Tibetan Rites, Explosive Calisthenics), not generic rep logging.
6. **Local + OSS + exe** — Rare combination for this category; good for privacy-conscious users and contributors.

## Where Integral is behind (honest gaps)

| Gap | Competitors doing it better |
|-----|----------------------------|
| Mobile apps | Daylio, LifeWheel, Benji |
| Cloud sync / multi-device | Benji, AuraOne, HALO |
| AI coaching | Orakemu, Sinqly, nopy |
| Social / accountability | Orakemu forums, Benji timelines |
| Wearable integration | Exist, Apple Health ecosystem |
| Polish / design budget | Funded SaaS products |
| Encryption at rest | Mini Diarium, Line by Line |

These gaps are tracked on the official roadmap: **[ROADMAP.md](ROADMAP.md)**.

| Roadmap priority | Gap | Target |
|------------------|-----|--------|
| Phase C | Cloud sync / multi-device | Optional encrypted sync (Benji parity, opt-in) |
| Phase D | AI coaching | Optional local/API-key AI (Orakemu, Sinqly, nopy parity) |
| Phase B | Encryption at rest | Passphrase vault (Mini Diarium parity) |
| Phase E | Brand / polish | Installer, onboarding, release assets (funded SaaS parity) |

Other opportunities: quarterly milestones, export/backup UX, mobile (not prioritized).

> **Integral** is a local-first desktop app for people building a whole life — not just a habit streak. Log honestly across the areas that matter, see your year at a glance, and get guidance when maintenance slips. No account. No cloud. Your data, your machine.
