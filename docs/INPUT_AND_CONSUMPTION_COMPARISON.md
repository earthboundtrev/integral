# Input & consumption tracking — how Integral builds it vs competitors

Part of Integral's broader **[holistic development model](HOLISTIC_DEVELOPMENT_MODEL.md)** — financial, physical, mental, emotional, spiritual, relational, cultural, and environmental life, plus what you take in.

Internal reference for how Integral’s **“what goes in”** life areas work, what each is for, and how dedicated apps in the same space approach the problem differently.

Last updated: July 2026.

---

## Why these exist in Integral

Most life trackers treat **outputs** (habits done, workouts, tasks) or **mood** as the unit of account. Integral also tracks **inputs** — what you ate, what art moved you, what you read, what you scrolled — because those patterns show up in burnout, creativity, body energy, and emotional wellbeing.

The design goal is **not** to replace Goodreads, Letterboxd, or MyFitnessPal. It is to log honestly in under a minute, see inputs next to the rest of your life on one grid, and get guidance when the balance looks off.

## Integral’s four input areas (default)

### 1. What You Have Eaten

**What it’s for:** Mindful awareness of food — not calorie accounting.

| Piece | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| **Checklist** | Logged meals/snacks, ate with awareness, choices aligned with intent |
| **Metrics** | Meals logged, nourishment rating, energy after eating |
| **Notes** | Free-form: what you ate, context, how it felt |
| **Guidance** | Neglected when body ratings are soft; declining nourishment tied to stress/burnout |

**Integral builds it as:** A daily **1–10 rating + optional ticks** tied to Body & Presence and Burnout — “how did food feel today?” not “did you hit macros?”

---

### 2. Art You Have Consumed

**What it’s for:** Aesthetic and emotional intake — things you experience as *art*, not reference material.

| Medium (checklist) | Examples |
|--------------------|----------|
| Film, TV, animation | Series, films, shorts |
| Music | Albums, concerts, deep listens |
| **Novels / literary fiction** | Novels, literary short fiction, poetry collections read for pleasure |
| Visual art, painting, comics | Galleries, graphic novels, illustration |
| Theatre / live performance | Plays, dance, live shows |
| Video games | Intentional play, not idle filler |

| Metrics | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Time with art | Rough hours |
| How much it moved me | Emotional impact |
| Would recommend | Separates memorable from passive consumption |

**Split from reading:** Fiction **novels** live here (art experience). Nonfiction, textbooks, and “I read to learn” live in **General Reading**.

**Integral builds it as:** One check-in for *any* art medium that day, with cross-links to Emotional Wellbeing when mood is low and no art is logged.

---

### 3. General Reading

**What it’s for:** Long-form text you read for information, craft, or reference — not primarily as aesthetic consumption.

| Piece | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| **Checklist** | Nonfiction, reference/textbook, memoir/biography, book-length audiobook, meaningful progress |
| **Metrics** | Reading time, pages/progress, what you retained or applied |
| **Notes** | Title, author, chapter, DNF reasons |

**Split from art:** Novels and literary fiction → **Art**. History, self-help, technical manuals, memoirs you’re studying → **General Reading**.

**Split from content:** Articles, newsletters, podcast episodes, TikTok essays → **Content**. Whole books → **General Reading**.

**Integral builds it as:** Progress and retention matter more than star ratings. Guidance flags “feeds up, books quiet” when short-form content dominates the week, and links low Creative/Mental Work to missing long-form input.

---

### 4. Content You Have Consumed

**What it’s for:** Shorter or feed-shaped intake — the stuff that’s easy to over-consume without noticing.

| Piece | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| **Checklist** | Articles/newsletters, podcasts, learning videos/courses, social/news (including doomscrolling), applied one idea |
| **Metrics** | Content time, quality of attention, actual value received |

**Integral builds it as:** Honest logging of **attention quality**, not just volume. Guidance when you log content often but attention trends down.

                    └──────────┬──────────┘

         ┌─────────────────────┼─────────────────────┐

## Competitors by input type

### Food & eating

| Product | How they build it | vs Integral |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| [MyFitnessPal](https://www.myfitnesspal.com/) | Barcode DB, calories/macros, goals, social | Deep nutrition science; cloud account; not holistic life context |
| [Lose It!](https://www.loseit.com/) | Calorie budget, meal scan, patterns | Same lane as MFP; subscription features |
| [Cronometer](https://cronometer.com/) | Micronutrient focus, precision logging | Best for biohackers; high friction |
| [Noom](https://www.noom.com/) | Psychology lessons + food logging + coaching | Behavior change program; expensive; not local |
| [Ate Food Journal](https://www.youate.com/) | Photo-first, mindful prompts, no calorie obsession | **Closest philosophy** to Integral’s food area — but mobile-only, food-only |
| [Recovery Record](https://www.recoveryrecord.com/) | Clinical eating-disorder support | Medical scope; not general life tracking |

**Integral angle:** Nourishment + energy ratings beside sleep and burnout — no food database, no shame metrics. Good for “how did eating feel today?” not “did I hit 1800 cal?”

---

### Art & media (film, TV, music, games — and novels as art)

| Product | How they build it | vs Integral |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| [Letterboxd](https://letterboxd.com/) | Film diary, lists, reviews, social | Gold standard for movies; no TV/music/life balance |
| [Trakt](https://trakt.tv/) | TV/film sync, scrobbling, lists | Auto-tracking from players; cloud; entertainment-only |
| [Serializd](https://www.serializd.com/) | TV episode logging, lists | TV-specific catalog |
| [Last.fm](https://www.last.fm/) / [ListenBrainz](https://listenbrainz.org/) | Music scrobbling, stats | Passive listening data; no reflection rating |
| [Backloggd](https://www.backloggd.com/) | Game library, reviews, lists | Games only; social catalog |
| [StoryGraph](https://app.thestorygraph.com/) | Books with mood/tags; recommendations | **Novels as art** — rich metadata; not tied to daily life areas |
| [Literal](https://literal.club/) | Aesthetic reading club, social | Book-as-art community; not local holistic journal |

**Integral angle:** One daily check-in across **all art media** including novels, with “moved me” / “would recommend” — not a public catalog or scrobbler. You tick what you engaged with and write one line in notes.

---

### Books & long-form reading (nonfiction / reference)

| Product | How they build it | vs Integral |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/) | Shelves, star reviews, yearly challenge, Amazon tie-in | **Largest book graph**; social; not daily wellbeing |
| [StoryGraph](https://app.thestorygraph.com/) | Mood, pace, tags; discovery | Better recommendations than GR; still catalog-centric |
| [Literal](https://literal.club/) | Clubs, curated lists | Social reading identity |
| [Bookly](https://getbookly.com/) | Reading sessions, streaks, stats, quotes | **Closest to Integral’s reading metrics** — time, pages, streaks — but books only |
| [Reading List](https://www.staycurious.co.nz/readinglist/) (various apps) | Simple TBR + finished | Lightweight; no life context |
| [Readwise](https://readwise.io/) | Highlight sync from Kindle/etc. | Retention/review of highlights; not “did I read today?” |

**Integral angle:** **General Reading** tracks progress and **retention/applied** — fiction novels go under Art. Cross-guidance when podcasts/feeds replace books. No ISBN database required.

---

### Short-form content (articles, podcasts, social, courses)

| Product | How they build it | vs Integral |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| [Pocket](https://getpocket.com/) / [Instapaper](https://www.instapaper.com/) | Save-for-later queue | Read later; no daily rating or life balance |
| [Matter](https://getmatter.com/) | Read-later + audio + highlights | Polished reader; subscription |
| [Feedly](https://feedly.com/) | RSS aggregation | Input firehose; no reflection layer |
| [Readwise Reader](https://read.readwise.io/) | Unified reader + highlights | Power-user tool; cloud |
| [Opal](https://www.opal.so/) / [One Sec](https://one sec.app/) | Screen-time blocks, friction | **Blocks** consumption; doesn’t journal it |
| [RescueTime](https://www.rescuetime.com/) | Passive app/site time | Automatic; no intentional “value received” rating |

**Integral angle:** You **self-report** attention quality and value — including doomscrolling. Integral doesn’t block feeds; it helps you see when volume is up and attention is down.

---

### All-in-one platforms (partial overlap)

| Product | Input tracking | vs Integral’s four areas |
|---------|----------------|--------------------------|
| [Benji](https://www.benji.so/) | Habits, notes, health modules | Generic habits — not art vs reading vs content split |
| [Daylio](https://daylio.net/) | Activity icons (incl. “eating”, “movies”) | Icons only; no nourishment metrics or book progress |
| [Exist](https://exist.io/) | Correlates passive data | Quantified self; not intentional art/reading logs |
| [HALO](https://halo.fit/) | Health + wealth modules | Module-based; not consumption philosophy |

## Design choices (why Integral is built this way)

| Choice | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| **Four areas, not one “Media” bucket** | Food, art, books, and feeds affect your life differently; guidance can be specific. |
| **Novels under Art, nonfiction under Reading** | Matches how people talk about “art I consumed” vs “books I’m working through.” |
| **No external APIs** | Works offline; no Goodreads/Letterboxd account required; title in notes is enough. |
| **Rating + checklist, not catalogs** | Optimized for maintenance and honesty, not building a public library. |
| **Cross-category insights** | Dedicated apps don’t see that body energy dropped the same week food wasn’t logged. |

---

## Where dedicated apps still win

| Need | Use |
|------|-----|
| Calorie/macro precision | MyFitnessPal, Cronometer |
| Film discovery & social diary | Letterboxd |
| Book recommendations & shelves | StoryGraph, Goodreads |
| Reading streaks & session timers | Bookly |
| Save-for-later reading queue | Pocket, Matter |
| Automatic screen-time truth | RescueTime, Opal |
| Passive music/film scrobbling | Last.fm, Trakt |

Integral is for people who want **inputs visible next to the rest of life** on one machine — not the best tool in any single vertical.
